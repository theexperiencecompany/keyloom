# Collaboration and Sharing

> Simple, responsive ways for people to share content and collaborate on it together while communicating with one another.

**When to use it:** For documents, boards, or content multiple people view or edit together, or any content people may want to send to others.

**Guidelines**
- Place a Share action in a consistent, convenient spot people expect, such as a toolbar or header.
- Offer familiar sharing destinations (copy link, message, email, social) and keep the choices minimal.
- Offer concise permission options in plain language ("Anyone with the link can view"); keep choices few and unambiguous.
- Let people both send a copy and collaborate live where each makes sense.
- Once collaboration starts, show a persistent, visible indicator of who is sharing or present near the Share control.
- Notify collaborators of relevant events (mentions, edits, joins) and bring them back to exactly the right view.

**Accessibility**
- Give share and collaboration controls clear, descriptive labels and a logical focus order.
- Announce presence and content changes to people relying on assistive technology, gently for routine updates.
- Don't identify collaborators by avatar color alone; include names or initials as legible text.
- Maintain sufficient contrast for presence indicators and sharing controls.

**Avoid**
- Burying the share entry point or overwhelming people with too many permission options.
- Ambiguous permission wording that hides who can see or edit content.
