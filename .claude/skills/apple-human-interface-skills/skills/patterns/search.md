# Search

> Lets people find content within your app, optionally scoped or filtered.

**When to use it:** For apps with enough content that browsing alone is slow, or where people need to filter and narrow a list by attributes.

**Guidelines**
- If search is important, give it a primary, persistent position such as a header, toolbar, or dedicated area.
- Provide one clearly identified place for global search; add scoped or local search per section only when it's genuinely distinct.
- Make the current scope obvious through placeholder text, scope labels, or a heading.
- Offer recent searches before people type, and suggestions, completions, and corrections while they type.
- Let people refine results with filters (date, type, size) shown near the results.
- Update results responsively as people type.

**Accessibility**
- Give the search field a visible or otherwise clear label, and make suggestions navigable without a pointer.
- Announce the number of results so people using assistive technology know the outcome of a search.
- Keep the field, suggestions, and results legible, high-contrast, and comfortably sized.

**Avoid**
- Hiding search behind an unmarked icon when search is central.
- Exposing search history where others may see it, with no way to clear it.
- Scattering content across multiple inconsistent search entry points.

**Full reference:** [full reference](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/searching)
