# Ratings and Reviews

> Asking people for feedback on your product at the right moment, without nagging.

**When to use it:** For prompting a rating or review after the person has had a genuinely positive, completed experience.

**Guidelines**
- Ask only after demonstrated engagement, such as completing a key task, reaching a milestone, or returning repeatedly; never on first visit or during onboarding.
- Time prompts to natural stopping points; don't interrupt an active task or flow.
- Don't pester: space requests well apart, cap how often you ask, and only re-ask after further engagement.
- Make the prompt lightweight, a single action to rate or dismiss, and never block the experience behind it.
- Let people easily opt out of future prompts; a great overall experience earns ratings better than aggressive prompting.

**Accessibility**
- Make a star input operable without a pointer and clearly labeled (for example, "Rate 4 of 5 stars").
- Ensure any prompt can be dismissed easily, with a clearly labeled close control and logical focus order.
- Don't convey the rating with color or icon alone; expose the value as legible text.

**Avoid**
- Requesting ratings on first launch, mid-task, or repeatedly.
- Prompts that can't be dismissed or that block progress.
