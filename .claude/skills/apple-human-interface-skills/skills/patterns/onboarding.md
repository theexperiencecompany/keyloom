# Onboarding

> A fast, optional flow that helps people get a quick start using your app.

**When to use it:** When the app isn't fully self-explanatory, a feature needs setup or permission before it works, or you want to surface new features in context.

**Guidelines**
- Prefer letting people learn by doing; make onboarding interactive rather than a series of read-only slides.
- Keep it brief, enjoyable, and skippable; don't force people to memorize anything.
- Favor context-specific tips near the relevant interface over one long upfront flow.
- If a tutorial is skippable, don't re-show it, but keep it findable later in help or settings.
- Provide sensible defaults so people can start immediately, and postpone non-essential setup.
- Tie a permission request to onboarding only when access is needed up front and you can explain the benefit.
- Let people experience the product before prompting for ratings, purchases, or accounts, and don't block on large downloads.

**Accessibility**
- Make every step navigable without a pointer, with a clear, reachable skip or dismiss control.
- Move focus to each step and announce it to people using assistive technology.
- Don't rely on hover-only coachmarks; ensure tips are reachable and dismissible without a pointer, and keep text legible and high-contrast.

**Avoid**
- Long, mandatory tutorials that overwhelm or can't be skipped.
- Teaching standard system behavior instead of your app's value.
- Showing the same tour repeatedly or burying legal text in the flow.
