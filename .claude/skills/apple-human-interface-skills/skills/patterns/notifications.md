# Notifications

> Timely, permission-gated messages that inform people of events, delivered respectfully without over-interrupting.

**When to use it:** For delivering relevant, time-appropriate updates such as messages, status changes, or security alerts, or transient in-app feedback.

**Guidelines**
- Ask permission before sending notifications, and ask in context after the person shows intent, not on first launch.
- Match urgency to content; reserve interrupting, high-priority delivery for genuinely time-sensitive events.
- Represent urgency honestly; inflating priority for low-value messages leads people to turn notifications off.
- Never use high-urgency channels for marketing; get explicit, separate opt-in for promotional messages with an easy way to opt out.
- Provide an in-app place to change or disable notification preferences.
- For transient in-app feedback, prefer non-blocking messages that dismiss on their own rather than a full system notification for everything.

**Accessibility**
- Announce in-app messages to people using assistive technology, gently for routine items and more assertively for urgent ones.
- Don't auto-dismiss critical messages so fast that people can't read or act on them, and keep any action easy to reach.
- Convey a notification's meaning with legible text and an icon, not color alone.

**Avoid**
- Prompting for permission on launch before showing any value.
- Marketing through notifications without consent, or inflating urgency to break through.
